## 1. Purpose and Scope

Formalizes risk containment and psychosocial security frameworks under the *Workplace Health and Safety Act*, explicitly recognizing the intense cognitive load native to directing automated LLM swarms.

---

## 2. Requirements or Procedures

### 2.1 Psychosocial Safety & Burnout Control
- Continuous "Agent Sprints" generate massive cognitive drain. Therefore, the Agency mandates a strict **15-minute complete detachment break** targeting every 2 continuous hours of deep, high-velocity coding or prompting.
- Systemic exhaustion warnings generated from agent clusters will result in automatic triage interventions from the Welfare framework.

### 2.2 Remote Ergonomics
- Physical hardware postures for developers and directors operating off-site must pass a formal `Home Office Ergonomic Checklist`. It is forbidden to maintain continuous operational command of the swarm from compromising physical vectors.

### 2.3 System Consultation
Significant shifts to the execution environment (e.g. migration to entirely new core inference models) must involve safety consultation windows measuring potential stress spikes on existing personnel.
