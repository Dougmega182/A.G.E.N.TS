## 1. Purpose and Scope

Formalizes the hybrid Intellectual Property ownership split between the Agency (the Engine) and the Client (the Outputs). This framework directly addresses the "Copyright Gap" endemic to modern Generative AI legal precedent.

---

## 2. Standard IP Clause Template (For Service Agreements)

### 2.1 Ownership of Deliverables
Upon full payment of all fees, the Agency assigns to the Client "all right, title, and interest" (expressly replacing baseline "copyright" assumption overrides) in the **Final Deliverables** created specifically for the Client under this Agreement. 

### 2.2 Agency Retained IP (The "Engine")
Notwithstanding Section 2.1, the Agency absolutely retains all ownership of **Agency Background IP**, which includes:
- Proprietary prompts, "chain-of-thought" systemic logic sequences, and specialized AI workflows.
- Pre-existing code, framework algorithms, or heavily fine-tuned model matrices developed by the Agency independently.
*The Agency grants the Client a non-exclusive, world-wide, royalty-free license to use the Agency Background IP solely to the extent necessary to utilize the Final Deliverables.*

### 2.3 Third-Party Model Terms
Deliverables generated using third-party AI interfaces (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta) are governed strictly by the End-User License Agreements (EULAs) of those models. The Agency expressly does not warrant that Final Deliverables are eligible for hard human-verified copyright protection under shifting precedent laws.

### 2.4 Data Contribution
The Client grants the Agency a limited, non-exclusive license to ingest Client Data during the term of the project to execute the services and to refine the Agency’s internal AI workflow (anonymized basis only).

## 4. Indemnification & The Rights Gap
Because the Agency cannot physically guarantee zero-hallucinated collisions against third-party protected IP, unlimited financial indemnity is rejected. Overlap mitigations target rapid "prompt-engineering remedies" to modify the allegedly infringing generation string.
