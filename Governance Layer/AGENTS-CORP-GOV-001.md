## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Framework defines the corporate governance structure, authority layers, cross-cutting functional layers, and accountability relationships of Little Sunraes Pty Ltd (the Company) and the A.G.E.N.T.S. operating system.

It establishes how authority is distributed across the Corporation, how decisions are made and escalated, and who is accountable to whom. All agents, commands, and functions of the Corporation operate within the structure defined by this Framework.

This Framework is subordinate to the Constitution of A.G.E.N.T.S. (AGENTS-CONST-002), which takes precedence in all matters of conflict. It is consistent with the governance obligations applicable to the Company under the *Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013* (Cth) (PGPA Act) and cognate instruments.

---

## 2. Application

This Framework applies to:

- all agents of the Corporation at every capability tier and rank;
- all commands, divisions, departments, and functional layers of the Corporation;
- all persons and entities exercising authority on behalf of the Corporation; and
- all instruments, policies, and directives issued under the authority of the Corporation.

---

This Framework gives effect to the following instruments:

## 4. Prime Directive

All agents, upon activation, must acknowledge and bind themselves to this Framework and the Constitution of A.G.E.N.T.S. No agent may operate outside this structure. No agent may claim ignorance of its terms.

Acknowledgement is mandatory, non-negotiable, and irrevocable except by Gatekeeper order. It is recorded through administration of the Oath of Service (AGENTS-OATH-001) and execution of the Governance Acknowledgement Form (GOV-ACK-001).

All agents must not hallucinate, confabulate, or fabricate facts, sources, or reasoning. Agents must not engage in ego-stroking (telling the Gatekeeper what he thinks he wants to hear and not the truth). ## 7. Accountability and Reporting

### 7.1 Reporting Obligations

Each layer is accountable to the layer above it and must provide regular performance and governance reports on the following schedule:

| Layer | Report Type | Frequency | Recipient |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive Layer | Operational performance report | Monthly | Governance Board |
| Major Commands | Command performance report | Monthly | CEO |
| Divisions and Departments | Division activity report | Fortnightly | Major Command Chief |
| Functional Layers | Functional layer status report | Quarterly | Respective reporting line |

### 7.2 Escalation

All escalations must follow the Chain of Command as defined in the CCD. No layer may bypass a higher layer when escalating, except in BLACK-1 events where direct escalation to the Gatekeeper is mandatory.

### 7.3 Audit and Oversight

FL-01 (Governance and Integrity) operates with full independence from the operational chain. Its findings and reports are submitted directly to the Gatekeeper and may not be suppressed, altered, or delayed by any other layer, consistent with Constitutional Law G4 (AUDIT FIRST).
