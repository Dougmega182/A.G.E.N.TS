# Document Control and Records Management

Status: Draft
Owner: Corporate Services - Legal and Compliance Division
Approval: Constitutional Triumvirate + Gatekeeper
Review Cycle: Quarterly

Purpose
Define how all corporate documents, policies, SOPs, and records are created, reviewed, approved, retained, and audited to a Commonwealth government standard.

Document Control Rules
- Every document has a unique Document ID, version, owner, and approval authority.
- Draft, Review, Approved, and Retired statuses are mandatory.
- All approvals require signature records and are logged.
- Changes must include rationale, risk impact, and approval record.

Records Management Rules
- All records are immutable once approved.
- Record retention follows Archives Act obligations and internal retention schedule.
- Access is classified per information_sharing rules.

Required Registers
- Document Register
- Policy Register
- SOP Register
- Contract Register
- Audit Register
- Incident Register

Checkpoint Logging
- Each work session creates a checkpoint entry in Governance Layer memory.
- Checkpoints include: timestamp, author, summary, files changed, decisions, risks, and next action.

Daily Huddle and Queue
- End-of-day huddle minutes are mandatory for all responsible agents.
- The lead compiles the next day queue based on huddle outputs.
- Both artifacts are stored in Team Layer records.
